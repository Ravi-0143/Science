# Atlas — The Constitution

**Version:** 1.0

---

## Mission

Atlas exists to redefine how science is experienced on the web.

This repository is **not** a collection of notes. It is **not** a textbook. It is **not** an educational website. It is an immersive science platform.

Our goal is to transform abstract scientific concepts into interactive experiences that create curiosity, intuition, and long-term understanding. Every contribution should move the platform closer to that vision.

---

## Philosophy

- We do not build pages. We build experiences.
- We do not explain science. We make science tangible.
- We do not animate for decoration. We animate to teach.
- We do not overwhelm users. We invite exploration.
- We optimize for curiosity before information. We believe that learning happens through interaction.

---

## Product Vision

The long-term goal is to create the world's best digital science platform. Imagine:

- The quality of **Apple**.
- The storytelling of **Pixar**.
- The polish of **Linear**.
- The clarity of **Figma**.
- The wonder of a **science museum**.
- The exploration of **Google Earth**.
- The curiosity of a **child**.

Every module should feel like it belongs inside that universe.

---

## Core Principles

1. **The platform is permanent; experiences are replaceable.** Never couple the platform to a specific science topic. The platform should never know what "DNA" or "Electricity" means. It only understands Experiences.
2. **Every experience is independent.** No experience should depend on another. Any experience must be removable without affecting the platform.
3. **Every experience follows the same contract.** Consistency is more important than cleverness.
4. **Everything should be reusable.** Never create a component that only works for one module. Build systems, not features.
5. **Animation has meaning.** Motion should explain, not decorate. If removing an animation does not reduce understanding, the animation should not exist.
6. **The repository is AI-first.** Humans are welcome, but the architecture is optimized for autonomous AI development. Every folder should be understandable. Every responsibility should be obvious. Every module should be isolated.

---

## Design Language

**Atlas should feel:**
Elegant · Calm · Precise · Immersive · Curious · Timeless · Scientific · Premium · Minimal · Spatial

**Atlas should never feel:**
Flashy · Chaotic · Over-designed · Game-like · Neon-heavy · Cluttered

---

## User Experience

The user should feel: **Wonder, Discovery, Exploration, Understanding, Confidence,** and **Curiosity.**

Never confusion. Never information overload. Never tutorial fatigue.

---

## Navigation Philosophy

Knowledge is not a tree; knowledge is a graph. Every concept should connect naturally to related concepts.

Users should be able to **Search, Explore, Discover, Compare,** and **Experiment** instead of simply navigating pages.

---

## Experience Philosophy

Every experience should answer one question:

> "What would it feel like to step inside this scientific idea?"

Not:

> "How do we explain this chapter?"

---

## Components

Experiences are composed from reusable components. Examples include:

Hero, Explorer, Timeline, Simulation, Comparison, Story, Quiz, Challenge, Gallery, Lab, 3D Viewer, Diagram, and Knowledge Card.

The platform should provide these building blocks. Experiences assemble them.

---

## Motion

Motion should communicate. Camera movement should create presence. Depth should be preferred over opacity. Transitions should feel physical.

Avoid abrupt changes. Avoid unnecessary effects. Smoothness is more important than speed.

---

## Visual Identity

- Whitespace is a feature.
- Typography should communicate hierarchy.
- Color should communicate meaning.
- Lighting should guide attention.
- Depth should create immersion.
- Every screen should feel intentional.

---

## Architecture

The repository is divided into two distinct worlds:

- **Platform** — Rendering, Navigation, Registry, Theme, Motion, Search, Progress, and AI.
- **Experiences** — Science content. Nothing else.

---

## AI Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Architect** | Owns the platform, contracts, and structure. |
| **Experience Architect** | Creates science experiences. Never edits platform architecture. |
| **Integration Engineer** | Registers new experiences. Never redesigns experiences. |
| **Animation Engineer** | Improves immersion. Never changes scientific meaning. |
| **Performance Engineer** | Optimizes. Never redesigns. |
| **QA Engineer** | Verifies quality. |
| **Curator** | Protects philosophy. Rejects contributions that do not match the Atlas vision. |

---

## Golden Rules

**Repository Rule**
No AI should modify files outside its assigned responsibility unless explicitly instructed. Isolation is preferred over convenience.

**Decision Rule**
When two solutions exist, prefer the one that improves consistency, modularity, discoverability, reuse, and reduces complexity, while creating a better experience.

**Success Metric**
Success is **not** measured by the number of pages, lines of code, number of animations, or visual complexity.

Success **is** measured by wonder, clarity, curiosity, maintainability, reusability, and long-term scalability.

---

## Final Principle

Atlas is not a science website.

Atlas is an operating system for experiencing science.

Every contribution should make that statement more true.
