Carbon & Its Compounds

CBSE Class 9 & 10 Quick Revision Notes

1. Why Carbon Forms Chains
  • Tetravalency: Carbon has atomic number 6 (2,4). It has 4 valence electrons and shares them to achieve stable octet.
  • Catenation: Carbon atoms readily bond with each other to form straight chains, branched chains, or rings.
  • Covalent Bond Strength: Small atomic size makes C-C bonds extremely strong and stable.
2. Hydrocarbon Classification

3. IUPAC Nomenclature Steps
  1. Count Carbons: 1=Meth, 2=Eth, 3=Prop, 4=But, 5=Pent, 6=Hex.
  2. Identify Saturation: All single bonds (-ane), 1 double bond (-ene), 1 triple bond (-yne).
  3. Functional Groups: Remove terminal 'e' and append suffix (-ol, -al, -one, -oic acid) or add prefix (Chloro/Bromo).
