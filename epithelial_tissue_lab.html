Diagram 1 • Single Layer

1. Simple Squamous

Oesophagus & Blood Vessels
Why: It is a single layer of ultra-flat tiles (like plastic wrap). Food needs to slide down smoothly without getting stuck in the oesophagus, and items pass through fast.
Diagram 2 • Multi-Layered

2. Stratified Squamous

Human Skin (Epidermis)
Why: "Stratified" means multi-layered. Your skin gets rubbed, scratched, and bumped all day long. One layer would break immediately, so nature stacks dozens of flat layers on top of each other like a deck of cards.
Diagram 3 • Cube Factories

3. Cuboidal Epithelium

Kidney Tubules & Salivary Glands
Why: These cells look like 3D boxes or dice. Because they are thick, they have space inside to hold the factory machinery needed to filter urine (kidneys) or secret saliva (salivary glands).
Diagram 4 • Tall Pillars + Broom Hairs

4. Ciliated Columnar

Respiratory Tract & Windpipe
Why: "Columnar" means tall like columns, and "Ciliated" means it has microscopic hairs (cilia) on top. When you breathe in dust, these hairs act like tiny brooms sweeping dirty mucus upwards out of your breathing tubes.
