Apical Meristem

Located at the growing tips of shoots and roots. Active cell division here drives Primary Growth, increasing the length of stems and roots.

Permanent Plant Tissues Summary (Post-Differentiation)

Parenchyma
Unspecialized living cells with thin cell walls & large intercellular spaces. Functions: Food storage, photosynthesis (Chlorenchyma), and buoyancy in aquatic plants (Aerenchyma).
Collenchyma
Living mechanical tissue with irregularly thickened corners (pectin & cellulose). Provides flexibility and mechanical support to leaf stalks and young stems without breaking.
Sclerenchyma
Dead cells at maturity with thick, lignified walls & no internal space. Provides extreme hardness and stiffness (e.g., coconut husk, seed coats, leaf veins).
Complex (Xylem / Phloem)
Vascular bundles for bulk transport: Xylem (Tracheids & Vessels) conducts water & minerals upward. Phloem (Sieve tubes & Companion cells) translocates food.

Cell Differentiation: Meristematic vs Permanent Cell

Meristematic Cell
Characteristics: Small isometric shape • Thin cellulose wall • Dense active cytoplasm • Prominent large nucleus • No vacuoles (actively dividing!).
Permanent Differentiated Cell
Characteristics: Specialized elongated/tubular shape • Thickened cell walls (lignin/pectin) • Large central vacuole • Loses power of cell division to perform specific permanent duties.
